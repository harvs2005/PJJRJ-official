Major Subject Every School Year

1.Technical Vocational Education and Training (TVET)

You can learn this at theFirst Year of School in PJJRJ, Technical Vocational Education and Training (TVET) is a type of education that focuses on providing individuals with the practical skills and knowledge needed to perform specific jobs and trades. TVET programs typically combine classroom instruction with hands-on experience in a particular field, such as engineering, construction, healthcare, automotive repair, or culinary arts. These programs are designed to prepare students for careers in skilled trades and technical occupations, and often lead to certifications or industry-specific qualifications. TVET is considered an important component of workforce development and can offer individuals the opportunity to gain valuable skills and secure employment in high-demand industries.

2.Internet Computing Fundamentals (ICF)

This is what you will learn in the Second Year of School in PJJRJ, Internet Computing Fundamentals is a field that focuses on the basic principles and concepts underlying the design, development, and implementation of internet-based applications and services. This includes topics such as networking, web technologies, web development, cloud computing, data communication, security, and internet protocols. The goal of internet computing fundamentals is to understand how the internet works and how to effectively utilize it to create and deploy software solutions that can be accessed and used over the internet.

3.Technical Drawing (TD)

You can learn this at theThird Year of School in PJJRJ, Technical Drawing is a specialized field within the broader field of engineering design and drafting. It involves the creation of detailed and accurate drawings that convey technical information, dimensions, and specifications for the construction, manufacturing, or assembly of various products, structures, or systems. Technical drawing is commonly used in industries such as engineering, architecture, construction, manufacturing, and automotive design to communicate complex, detailed information in a visual format. Technical drawings can include 2D and 3D representations, schematics, diagrams, blueprints, and detailed plans that help guide the development and production of products and structures.

4.Architechture Fundamentals (AF)

You can learn this at theFourth Year of School in PJJRJ, Architecture Fundamentals is a broad field that encompasses the basic principles and concepts of designing and constructing buildings and other physical structures. This includes understanding spatial relationships, structural systems, materials, building codes, environmental factors, and aesthetic considerations. The fundamentals of architecture form the foundation for all architectural design work and are essential for creating well-designed and functional buildings.
